Get data about government services

Cross-Government Service Data lets civil servants quickly access standardised high-level data so they can:

  • get an overview of all the services delivered by government
  • see how departments, agencies and services are related
  • track changes in service usage and delivery
View data  or search for something specific

What data is available

Where appropriate data is also aggregated to the agency, department and UK government level.

Information about services

  • The purpose of services
  • The typical users of services
  • How services works
  • How frequently services are used
  • How long it takes for transactions to end in an outcome

Demand for services

  • Transactions received by services

Channels people use to access services

  • Online
  • Phone
  • Paper
  • Face-to-face

Delivery of services

  • Transactions ending in an outcome
  • Transactions ending in the user's intended outcome (where available)

Reasons people call services

  • To get information
  • To chase progress
  • To challenge a decision

Guidance about how metrics are collected and calculated

  • Department and agency responsible for the data
  • How the data was collected
  • When the data was last updated

How it works

Cross-Government Service Data is designed for civil servants who work across or want to learn from multiple organisations within government.

It provides standardised high-level metrics along with additional context to help them get a rounded view of each service, agency and department.

This data can be explored through the Cross-Government Service Data website or exported as a CSV.

All metrics also come with guidance on how they were collected and calculated to allow easy evaluation of the data.

Contact the team

Cross-Government Service Data is collected by the Government Digital Service.
