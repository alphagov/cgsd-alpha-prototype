Guidance Reasons users call services

Many users need additional help to achieve their goal and providing this is an integral part of the service.

Collecting this data helps government discover unmet user needs and opportunities to reduce waste.

When users call a service their calls (or a sample of them) are sorted into the following categories:

User needs more information to use the service

This includes:

  • couldn't find what they needed online
  • couldn't understand our website
  • needs to know more about something

User wants an update on the progress of their transaction

This includes:

  • wants to know what's happened to information they've previously supplied
  • wants to know if something is still valid
  • wants to know why they haven't heard back about something

User wants to challenge the outcome of their transaction

This includes:

  • wants to appeal a decision
  • has been sent incorrect information

User has accessibility or language needs

This includes:

  • is unable to use the service due to a disability
  • isn't confident using a computer
  • doesn't have access to a computer

User was asked to call by the service

This includes:

  • was asked to get in contact as part of the service
  • contacting the service is the only way to do what they want to do
